Quantum Tunneling
Unstable/Damage
Quantum tunneling allows protons to bypass energy barriers, causing temporary structural changes (tautomeric shifts) in DNA bases. This leads to mispairing during replication -> the primary cause of mutations.

ROS Production and DNA Damage

Mitochondrial dysfunction produces reactive oxygen species (ROS) that damage DNA directly and destabilise hydrogen bonds, increasing quantum tunneling probability and mutation rates.

ROS-Quantum Damage Cascade

Reactive oxygen species disrupt quantum electron fields around DNA, increasing tunneling probability by 1000x. This creates a self-amplifying cycle: more damage → more ROS → more quantum instability → exponentially increasing mutation rates.

Radiation-Induced Quantum Disruption

Ionising radiation excites electrons to higher energy states, disrupting molecular orbitals and potentially causing quantum decoherence in DNA base pairs, leading to increased mutation rates.

From Quantum Events to Cancer

The quantum pathway to cancer: from femtosecond tunneling events through accumulated mutations to cancer, influenced by ROS and radiation.

Quantum Energy Landscape

Classical physics predicts protons cannot cross high energy barriers, but quantum mechanics allows tunneling through barriers via wave-particle duality.

Quantum Coherence vs Decoherence in Cellular Systems

Biological systems exploit quantum coherence for enhanced efficiency but must manage environmental decoherence. Cancer disrupts this balance, destroying beneficial quantum effects while amplifying harmful quantum mutations.
